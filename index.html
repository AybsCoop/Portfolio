BattleShip Project

This was a team project for the CMPS 270 course at the American University of Beirut. I played a leading role and carried the following main jobs:

  • took complete responsibility for planning, breaking down work into smaller chunks, and distributing jobs on teammates
  • came up with the solution to creating a human-like bot by giving the bot multidimensional analytical skills
  • combined my knowledge in algorithms, data structures, and time complexity analysis to come up with solutions for time efficiency, and data management
  • emphasized deeply on forward-looking development and pushed the team to focus on code scalability and modularization


I was extremely lucky to be with a very hardworking team who were always ready to put in the extra effort and make some of my at-first-glance-crazy ideas come to life. But in the end we made it work and got an A on the project.

The project is coded entirely in C. It involves very good software engineering practices as well as implementations of various data structures and algorithms from scratch.

Clumsy Thief

One of the most challenging projects I have ever attempted.

After dozens of failed unfinished games, I decided to finally follow a more professional and systemetic approach to developing this project.

Unlike previous projects I'd done, this time around:

  • I researched A LOT about good software engineering practices (even read an entire book by Robert Nystrom called Game Design Patterns) and learned and implemented various new concepts, such as:
    • Abstraction
    • Modularization
    • Design Patterns
    • Optimization Patterns
  • I studied and implemented various data structures and algorithms both for efficiency reasons and in preparation for upcoming semester:
    • Heaps for storing the security camera's detected threats by priority
    • QuickSort was used to sort pickable items by distance and how centered they were in player camera's viewport
    • A* algorithm for calculating the path of a Rumba that cleans the room
    • etc...
  • I learned how to test and optimize the performance of my algorithms and systems as well as their correctness.
  • I learned to thoroughly read documentations and understand how functions in Unity libraries actaully worked and how they would impact both the performance and correctness of my code.
